Innovation
starts with STEAM.

STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Math) education is widely touted as the educational path to innovation, but it is the arts – broadly defined – that are poised to transform our economy in the 21st century like science and technology did in the last century.

Technology has become a commodity, and art and design are the new differentiators. Access to ever-increasing data sets, complex scientific discovery, and always-on devices have left us oversaturated with information. Artists and designers make this information more understandable, products more desirable, progress more rooted in our own

Creativity and ingenuity have always been central to the American story of progress, as has our history of making things. Look at Apple's success with the iPhone - there were loads of smart phones before it, but none changed our behavior so radically until Apple took a design-centered approach. Silicon Valley has begun to recognize that it’s designers that make the difference for startups success and we have seen the rise of the designer founder, like Pinterest, Airbnb, another example.

Creativity
is a skill.

The problem-solving, the fearlessness, and the critical thinking and making skills that define an art and design education fosters the creativity that will keep our country innovating, and their development needs to start in the K-12 schools. Creativity is the new literacy, and we need a well-rounded education system that develops the skills our economy needs.

Sustaining arts education in its own right remains critically important. But equally important is taking a page from schools that have been successful at integrating the arts into STEM curriculum to keep students engaged . Hands-on, project based, inquiry -driven education -- interdisciplinary and rooted in making -- are the hallmarks of STEAM education, ensuring that students will be ready to become innovators of tomorrow.
