Innovation
starts with STEAM.

{% include svg/title-illustration.html %} {% include svg/orb.html %}

STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Math) education is widely touted as the educational path to innovation, but the arts – broadly defined – are poised to transform our economy in the 21st century like science and technology did in the last century.

Technology has become a commodity, and art and design are the new differentiators. Creativity and ingenuity have always been central to the American story of progress, as has our history of making things. Artists and designers make information more understandable, products more desirable, and progress rooted in our own values.

Creativity
is a skill.

The problem-solving, the fearlessness, and critical thinking and making skills that define an art and design education fosters the creativity that will keep our country innovating, and their development needs to start in the K-12 schools. Creativity is the new literacy, and we need a well-rounded education system that develops the skills our economy needs.

Sustaining arts education in its own right remains critically important. But equally important is taking a page from schools that have been successful at integrating the arts into STEM curriculum to keep students engaged. Hands-on, project based, inquiry -driven education — interdisciplinary and rooted in making — are the hallmarks of STEAM education, ensuring that students will be ready to become innovators of tomorrow.

Join Us!

STEAM is a movement, championed by the Rhode Island School of Design, arguing that an art and design education will produce the creativity and innovation that will keep America competitive in this century.

STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering and Math) + Arts = STEAM.
